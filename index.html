01 / About

About

I'm a graduate engineer at UC Berkeley finishing an MS in Mechanical Engineering (May 2026), with a BS in Mechanical Engineering and an EECS minor completed Dec 2024. My work sits at the intersection of mechanical hardware, embedded firmware, and digital logic — the kind of full-stack systems engineering that defense and aerospace platforms run on.

I build pipelined RISC-V CPUs on Xilinx FPGAs, write FreeRTOS firmware for real-time attitude control, design buck/boost power electronics and PCBs, and ship the operating-system kernels and compilers that run on top. I hold an active DoD Secret clearance from a summer at the Naval Air Warfare Center Weapons Division, where I worked on phased-array beam-steering and RF signal processing.

I'm looking for hardware–software systems roles at defense primes or defense-adjacent startups — places where firmware lives next to silicon, latency budgets are measured in microseconds, and the system has to actually fly, sail, or steer.
