01 / About

About

I'm a recent graduate of UC Berkeley (May 2026), where I studied Mechanical Engineering alongside Electrical Engineering & Computer Sciences (EECS). My work sits at the exact intersection of traditional mechanical control, modern computer vision, and systems-level software — the foundational stack required for next-generation robotics and autonomous platforms.

My engineering background spans both the physical and the digital. On the hardware and systems side, I build custom RISC-V processor cores on FPGAs, develop kernel-level process management for operating systems, build compilers from scratch, and rapidly prototype robotic systems integrating microcontrollers and sensors with 3D-printed mechanical structures. On the software and AI side, I work with advanced computer vision models, utilizing architectures like V-JEPA for latent-space trajectory prediction and intuitive physics.

I'm actively searching for full-time roles in robotics, control systems, or hardware–software integration. I thrive in environments that bridge the gap between mechanical engineering and computer vision, and I'm passionate about building systems where intelligent, low-latency software interfaces directly with physical hardware to solve complex, real-world problems.
